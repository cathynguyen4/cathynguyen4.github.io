Pacific Rhododendron

Rhododendron macrophyllum — Washington State Flower

Quick Reference

The Pacific rhododendron is a broadleaf evergreen shrub known for its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of pink, bell-shaped flowers. It grows naturally in forests and coastal mountain regions of the Pacific Northwest.

  • Scientific name: Rhododendron macrophyllum
  • Common names: Pacific rhododendron, Coast rhododendron, Bigleaf rhododendron
  • Plant type: Evergreen shrub

Physical Size

Typically grows as a large shrub and can reach around 6–20 feet tall, depending on growing conditions.

Geographic Range

Native to the Pacific Northwest, especially western Washington and Oregon, and found from coastal areas into nearby mountain regions.

Best Climate

Does best in cool, moist climates with mild temperatures. It prefers partial shade and well-draining, slightly acidic soil.

Where You Might See It

Common along forest edges, shaded woodlands, and coastal mountain forests. You’ll often see it in spring when it blooms.
