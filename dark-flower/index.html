Pacific Rhododendron

Washington’s State Flower

The Pacific rhododendron is a flowering shrub native to the Pacific Northwest. It is known for its soft pink to purple blossoms and thick evergreen leaves. This plant grows naturally in forested areas and is especially common in Washington, Oregon, and British Columbia.

Scientific Name

Rhododendron macrophyllum

Habitat

It thrives in wooded slopes, coastal forests, and mountain areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Fun Fact

The Pacific rhododendron was named the official state flower of Washington in 1892.

Why It Matters

The Pacific rhododendron is important because it represents the natural beauty of the Pacific Northwest. Its bright blooms add color to forest landscapes, and it supports local ecosystems by providing shelter and attracting pollinators.
