Designing as Visual Organization

“…when the matter of usefulness is involved… forms… are intrinsically geometric.”

It is safe, I suppose, to assume that today most if not all of us have had the experience of looking down from an airplane...

Or on a beach, we may find a button, a bottle...

To turn from “looking at” to action...

If, then, it appears that our stamp is lucidity...

“Culture, surely, is measured by art…”

About the Author

Anni Albers was a Bauhaus-trained textile artist whose work bridged craft and modern design...

Her philosophy centered on clarity, structure, and thoughtful form, making her one of the most important figures in modern design theory.
